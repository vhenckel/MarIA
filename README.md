# MarIA — AI for Women’s Safety

MarIA is an open-source initiative that aims to use Artificial Intelligence and public data to help prevent violence against women.

The project focuses on organizing and analyzing publicly available information to make safety resources more accessible, improve awareness, and help women find support services when they need them most.

The name **MarIA** references both **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** and **Maria da Penha**, a landmark Brazilian law created to combat domestic violence against women.

---

## Why this project exists

Brazil has faced a concerning increase in cases of domestic violence and femicide in recent years.

Access to reliable safety information and support services is often fragmented across many institutions and websites. Many women do not know where to find help or how to identify risk situations.

MarIA aims to help bridge this gap by using technology to organize and make critical information easier to access.

---

## Project goals

MarIA aims to build an open platform that:

• Aggregates public data related to violence prevention and support services  
• Makes safety resources easier to discover and understand  
• Uses AI to analyze patterns and improve access to relevant information  
• Provides tools that help women identify support networks and available resources  
• Promotes responsible and ethical use of public data  

---

## What MarIA will provide

The project intends to develop:

### Safety Information Platform

A web application that centralizes:

- Support hotlines
- Women's protection services
- Public safety resources
- Institutional information
- Legal guidance and reporting channels

### AI-assisted Data Organization

AI models will help:

- Structure and summarize large public datasets
- Improve discoverability of relevant information
- Provide contextual safety guidance
- Support multilingual access to resources

### Public Data Integration

MarIA will integrate multiple public datasets such as:

- Public safety statistics
- Government transparency data
- Institutional support services
- Legal and policy documentation

All integrations will prioritize **privacy, ethics, and responsible use of public information**.

---

## Ethical principles

MarIA is committed to responsible technology.

The project will:

- Use only **publicly available or authorized datasets**
- Respect **privacy and data protection regulations**
- Avoid exposing personal data improperly
- Focus on **prevention, awareness, and support**

The goal is to empower people with information, not to create systems that can be misused.
